FAQ

General Event Questions

What is a hackathon?
A hackathon is a fun collaborative development session where people work together to build cool ideas. Entrepreneurs, designers, and developers work in teams to create cool apps or prototypes for web or mobile platforms.

But Blueprint is not just a hackathon.
So how is Blueprint different?
Blueprint is a Pilot event, a sort of combination of a hackathon and a 14-hour workshop focused on creativity, collaboration, and learning by doing. Our event is for high school students of all skill levels—it’s totally fine if you’ve never programmed before! All you need is interest and creativity; our team of world-class mentors will help you learn the rest. You’ll get to meet mentors from a variety of innovative companies, as well as talk to entrepreneurs involved in the startup scene.
If you are more experienced, you’ll be able to focus on building your idea with your team, and consult with our expert mentors as needed.
Who can participate?
Blueprint is open to all high school students! Coding experience is not required.
What if I don't live in the Boston area?
You can still sign up for Blueprint, and we encourage you to do so! However, given our focus on local schools, we will not be reimbursing travel expenses.
How much does it cost?
Nothing. Blueprint is completely free, thanks to HackMIT as well as our amazing sponsors.
What if I don't know how to code?
Don't worry—you definitely won't be alone and, as long as you're interested and willing to learn, we'll teach you all you need to know over the course of the day.
Do I need a team?
Nope! We will have sessions during check-in to help people find team members and form teams, as well as an idea generation workshop to help people think of ideas to work on for Blueprint.
Will there be food?
There will be a lot of great, healthy food. (Clover, anyone?) We will also ensure to cater to your food allergies and dietary restrictions as specified during registration.
How does judging work?
Our panel of judges will rate your app based on creativity, technical difficulty, polish, and usefulness. Companies will be sponsoring prizes in a variety of categories. Exact details TBA.
Can I start my project before the event?
No. To make sure everyone has an equal opportunity at building a project, you may not begin your project until the start of Blueprint (8AM on February 22, 2014).

Day-of Event Info

How do I check-in?
Check-in will be from 8–9AM on the ground floor of the building. All you need is a valid photo ID (name must match registration). If you don't have one, you can bring a high school transcript or report card. If you don't have that, contact us.
How long is the event?
Doors open at 8AM, Kick-off at 9AM.
The event ends at 11PM. Detailed schedule TBA.
Can I bring my parents?
Unfortunately, we don't have enough space to accommodate parents. However, we will broadcast our closing ceremony online—we’ll send out the link beforehand.
What should I bring?
Laptop and charger. All attendees are required to bring a valid photo ID for admission.
Do I have to be there the entire time?
For security reasons, you must stay in the event space for the duration of the event. If your parent or guardian needs to pick you up before the end, he or she will need to contact event staff and check you out through our registration system.
I have a question that's not listed here. Who should I ask?
Email us at blueprint@hackmit.org.
